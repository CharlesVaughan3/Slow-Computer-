------------------------------SLOW Startup Speed----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This document contains the steps that I have found are the best to take to optimize startup speed. 

The best thing to do in this case is minimize the applications that start on startup
1. Disable startup applications
	i. use start menu search to locate the task manager > click startup (one of the tabs) > go through the programs
	   and determine what you might not need. Anything falling under that category should be right clicked and disabled
	ii. You can always re enable these and you can also search the startup processes by the impact they have (high being
	    the most impactful). 

2. Changing boot timeout
	i. use start menu search to find the run tool > type msconfig and hit enter > click on the boot tab > change
	   the timeout from 30 seconds to 10 seconds. This will not have a significant impact but will shave a few seconds
	   off the boot time

3. Defragment your hard drive
	i. Check with IT to do this

4. Check if fast startup is enabled
	i. get to control panel > click system and security > poewr options > choos what the power buttons do(left side)
	   > click change settings that are currently unavailable link > make sure the box next to " turn on fast startup(recommended)
	   is checked. 

To read more about these steps and trick check out the following links
